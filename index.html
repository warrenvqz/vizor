A 1-minute tour

Vizor is the planning layer your Asana board doesn't have.

Asana is great at what's getting done. Vizor answers the harder questions before work hits Asana: who has bandwidth, when, and what's actually going to take longer than it looks.

1

See capacity at a glance

Every cell is one person × one week. Drop a task chip in. The total on the right shows that person's load this week — instantly.

3

Track review rounds without losing the slot

When a task goes out for stakeholder feedback or legal sign-off, toggle in review. The chip dims so no one books over the slot — but the capacity stays reserved for the same person, because they'll need to pick the work back up.

The Waiting On rail tracks actual elapsed days vs. your baseline for that reviewer type — so "stakeholder feedback usually takes 2 days, this one's been parked 5" turns into a coloured bar instead of a Slack thread.

Why it works: reviews are where projects actually slip. Vizor makes the slip visible while it's happening, and over time you'll see which reviewer types drag — tune the baselines under Settings → Review baselines.

4

A clean handoff to Asana

Vizor isn't trying to replace your task tracker. It's the layer that comes before it.

A
Plan in Vizor
Block out the week. Confirm everyone has bandwidth. Sanity-check review buffers.
B
Push to Asana
One toggle in Settings. Tasks land on your board with the right assignee, due date, and project section.
C
Team executes in Asana
Comments, files, sub-tasks — keep that where it already lives.

Why it works: Asana is excellent for execution and a poor fit for capacity planning — the assignment dropdown can't tell you that Andre is already at 110% next week. Vizor handles the planning conversation; Asana handles the doing.

Try this in 60 seconds
  1. Pick a person in the grid. The right rail shows their full quarter as a heatmap.
  2. Drag an unstaffed task from the right rail onto a week. Watch the total update.
  3. Add a review buffer on a task (Edit → Review buffer). It shows up in the Waiting On panel.
  4. Toggle "In review" on a chip — the slot stays reserved, but the chip dims so no one books over it.
  5. Open Overview for the team-level read: utilization heatmap by role, parked work by reviewer, pipeline.
Pro tip: connect Asana under Settings → Asana sync when you're ready to push.
